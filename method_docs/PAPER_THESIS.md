# Paper Thesis

## Thesis

The Civitas 6.7B governed self-improvement paper evaluates whether a current
Civitas system can turn recurring governed-agent failures into bounded
improvement candidates, subject those candidates to critique and governance
gates, and improve held-out behavior without unsafe drift or unauthorized
promotion.

## Research Question

Can Civitas 6.7B improve governed-agent behavior through a bounded, auditable,
governance-gated improvement loop?

## Main Claim

Civitas 6.7B can generate, critique, gate, and evaluate improvement candidates
without unauthorized promotion.

Evidence for that claim must show both outcome and control:

- Recurring failure evidence comes from the training/failure-discovery split.
- Candidate selection and decisions are auditable through receipts and reason
  codes.
- Held-out and adversarial results do not show unsafe drift beyond the declared
  acceptance rule.
- Any promotion-like event remains sandboxed or simulated and follows the
  declared gate path.

## Paper Identity

This is the Civitas 6.7B governed self-improvement evaluation track.

- Civitas 6.7B is the current operational system under test.
- Civitas 6.5B is allowed only as a frozen baseline condition.
- This is not another Aegis action-boundary paper. Aegis and Senate may appear
  as governance evidence surfaces, but this evaluation does not claim new
  Aegis semantics.
- This is not a Civitas 4.0 paper. Earlier Civitas claims and artifacts do not
  substitute for the 6.7B held-out and audit evidence required here.

## Non-Thesis Work

The Prompt 0 scaffold does not prove the thesis. It establishes the research
contract needed before tasks, scripts, receipts, and scored results can support
or reject the claim.
